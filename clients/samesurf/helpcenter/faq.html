What's Samesurf?

Samesurf is an install-free co-browsing platform that empowers multiple people to experience the same content in real-time from virtually any device or browser. Our suite of products transcend alternative offerings such as screen sharing in terms of simplicity, security and performance.

How are Screen Sharing and Samesurf Co-Browsing Different?

Screen sharing broadcasts the contents of the presenter's screen to other users by continuously capturing and broadcasting mini-snapshots of that person's screen. This form of real-time sharing (1) is CPU, GPU and bandwidth intensive; (2) requires installs; (3) is considerably slower than Samesurf Co-browsing in terms of rendering speed; (4) amounts to a one-way non-interactive experience; and (5) is ineffective on mobile.

Samesurf Co-Browsing synchronizes the pages that the leader and followers visit during their shared browsing experience by simulating the events that occur within those pages on both sides. This patented form of real-time sharing serves as a highly effective alternative to screen sharing systems that (1) requires considerably less CPU, GPU and bandwidth usage versus screen sharing; (2) requires no installs of any kind for virtually all devices; (3) is considerably faster than screen sharing in terms of rendering speed; and (4) constitutes a multi-way interactive experience that empowers both sides to interact with online content in real-time. For a more in-depth comparison, please visit the Samesurf vs Screensharing page.

What is a Room?

A room is an install-free session that enables one interchangeable leader to guide any number of followers through a shared experience. Within a room, users can follow the leader from page to page, audio and video chat, text chat, screen draw, track cursors, collaborate, pass leader controls and toggle into screen sharing mode to display offline content.

What is Sameslide?

Sameslide empowers multiple users to deliver and view pristine presentations from any device without enduring installs, fuzzy screens or latency. It enables presenters to easily fuse co-browse ready slides with crystal clear video and site sharing to simulate the experience of being together in real life.

What are Public Rooms?

Public Rooms are open sessions that enable multiple users to co-browse websites and videos with people from across the globe.

What are Private Rooms?

Samesurf Private Rooms are invite-only sessions made for smaller groups who choose to limit their participants to certain individuals.

What is a Host?

A host is the person who started the room or inherited this designation after the original host left the room. The host has the ability to perform special functions like passing or taking back leader controls and disconnecting specific users from the room.

What is a Leader?

A leader is the interchangeable person who guides the room participants from page to page throughout the shared browsing session.

What is a Follower?

A follower is the user who trails the leader from page to page in real-time. He or she can become the leader by requesting and then being granted control from either the host or current leader.

How Do I Start a Room?

You can start a room in the following ways:

(1) For Individuals: Log into your Samesurf account and click the "Start" button to initiate the room.

(2) For Support & Sales: Log into your Samesurf account and trigger the "Start" button for Phone Support or click the Start Button within the Samesurf Agent Console for Live Chat (coming soon).

(3) For Websites: Activate the integrated Start button on the Samesurf enabled site and click "Start New Room" from the pop-up modal.

(4) For the Desktop and Mobile App: Simply click the "Start" button.

(5) Switch Rooms Button: Select this option from the Tools Menu if you're already in a room and you'll be prompted to start or join an existing room.

How Do I Invite Others to Join a Room?

You can send invites by:

(1) Copying and pasting the invite link into any chat or email window;

(2) Sending a web invite via Samesurf Mail or through your own default email service;

(3 Using the Invite button within the Samesurf Social Chat window;

(4) Posting the invite link on Facebook, Google+ or Twitter via Samesurf Social Chat for mass invites; or

(5) Sending a text message invite via a mobile device (coming soon).

How Many People can Co-Browse Together within the Same Room?

The number of people who can co-browse together is dependent on the permissions that relate to your particular Samesurf account. However and speaking purely from a technical standpoint, we have successfully tested 5000 simultaneous users for co-browsing within a single session.

How Do I Join a Room?

You can join a room by clicking an invite link or by entering the unique room identifier into the Join a Room box on a Samesurf enabled site.

Do I have to Download or Accept any Installs, Plugins or Downloads to use Samesurf Co-Browsing?

No.

Does Samesurf Co-Browsing rely on any Dependent Software like Java or Flash?

No.

Does Samesurf Require Installs for Audio and Video Chat on a Desktop Browser?

On supported browsers like Google Chrome and Opera, we leverage webRTC technology to enable install-free audio and video chat in high definition. For browsers like Internet Explorer and Safari which don't yet support webRTC, we leverage and require Flash 11 (or higher) for utilzing audio and video chat.

Does Samesurf Work on all Browsers and Devices?

Samesurf works on virtually every browser including Internet Explorer 8 (and higher), Google Chrome, Firefox, Safari and Opera. Within certain corporate environments, IE8 users may require Flash 12+ since IE8 utilizes less robust web sockets technology. In terms of device support, Samesurf is optimized for Windows, Mac, iOS and Android.

Am I Required to Login using a Service like Facebook, Google+ or Twitter?

No but if you do login with these integrated services, you can chat, update your status and send instant invites to your online friends with a single click from the Samesurf Social Chat Window.

Does Samesurf Post to my Wall or Send Tweets without my Knowledge?

No.

Does Samesurf Gain Access to my Basic Social Networking Information when I Log into Samesurf with my Facebook, Twitter or Google+ Account?

Yes but only to retrieve your profile information in order to allow you to chat, send messages and transmit one-click invites to your online friends.

Am I Required to Login using a Service like Facebook, Google+ or Twitter to utilize Samesurf?

No but if you do login with these integrated services, you can chat, update your status, send Tweets and transmit instant invites to your online friends with a single click from the Samesurf Social Chat Window.

Where do the Room Features Reside?

The list of available features flows from the Features button that is positioned on the Samesurf Topbar.

What do the Social Chat Functions do?

These functions enable you to log into your social accounts on services like Facebook, Google and Twitter so you can chat with online friends, send invites, update your status and transmit Tweets directly from Samesurf or Samesurf enabled sites.

How do I Request, Pass or Take Back Leader Controls?

To request control, use the Control button to prompt the current leader to either give you control or deny your request.

To pass control as the leader, click the Control button and select a new leader from the user list.

To take back control as the host, click the Control button and click Yes to take back control.

How does Screen Drawing Work?

If the leader turns Drawing on or off, this automatically triggers Draw mode on or off for the followers as well. For drawing to appear on the follower side, the leader must turn on Screen Drawing and actually make a screen marking before draw mode is activated on the follower side.

A follower can independently draw on the screen but unless the leader has turned on Screen Drawing via the Tools Button on the Samesurf Topbar, the follower's drawings will not be seen by the other participants.

What is Mouse Tracking?

Mouse tracking enables everyone in the room to view each other's cursors in real-time. If a leader scrolls up or down within a page, the followers will also auto-scroll to match the leader's movements on that page.

What does the Switch Rooms Feature do?

It enables any person to leave an existing room and start or join a new one.

How do I Reenter the Room if I get Disconnected?

The process for re-joining a room is the same as the initial one for joining that room. For more information, visit How Do I Join a Room?

How do I Send Public and Private Messages to Other People in the Room?

After clicking the Chat button from the Samesurf Topbar, you will be able to send messages to everyone in the room if the in-room chat module is set "To All." In order to send a private message, select a specific user from the dropdown list and send your message to that one person.

Can I be in Two or More Active Co-Browse Sessions within Different Browser Tabs at the Same Time?

The ability to co-browse within multiple rooms depends on your Samesurf Account Subscription. Please check the Pricing Page for additional information on whether you're eligible to run simultaneous co-browse sessions.

What does the List in Public Rooms Feature do?

This opt-in selection lists your room within our public rooms directory at PublicRooms.com so it's easier for others to find and join your room.

What are Samesurf Private Rooms?

Samesurf Private Rooms are invite-only sessions made for smaller groups who choose to limit their participants to certain individuals.

How many People can Audio and Video Chat Together within a Single Room?

We currently support 1-on-1 HD audio and video chat on browsers that support webRTC. We are now working to increase the number of simultaneous users that we can support for this functionality.

For browsers that don't yet support webRTC like Internet Explorer and Safari, we support flash based audio-video chat (Flash 11+ required).

Do Videos Play in Unison on Enabled Sites like PublicRooms.com so they Play, Pause and Skip Together in Real-Time?

We support this functionality on enabled sites like PublicRooms.com. However and if you're browsing another video site, this functionality is not yet supported. In order to view videos in perfect synchronicity with other users, please visit PublicRooms.com where you'll have the ability to co-browse videos from the robust Youtube and Vimeo libraries.

How do I Share Applications and other Offline Content that Resides on my Desktop?

As the leader, you can click the Start Screen Sharing button that flows from the Features Button on supported browsers to enable others to see your entire screen or the portion that lies within the dynamically adjustable red box.

Does Screen Sharing Work on Mobile Phones and Tablets?

You can share your screen from a desktop computer into virtually any mobile phone or tablet without requiring installs of any kind on the viewer side. However, you currently can't initiate a screen sharing broadcast from a mobile device. We're happy to report that we are now in the process of perfecting the ability to screen share from a mobile device and will make this feature available within the coming months.

What is Required to Initiate the Screen Sharing Function on my Computer?

On Windows, you must have Java (version 1.6+) installed on your computer to broadcast your screen to other participants. If you don't already have Java, Samesurf will auto-detect this factor and walk you through on how to install Java on your computer in order to initiate screen sharing.

On Mac, there is a one-time Samesurf install for sharing your screen. You'll note that there are no installs required of any kind to view screen sharing transmissions on a smartphone, tablet, computer or web appliance.

Is there an Install Requirement on the Viewer Side during Screen Sharing?

No. Samesurf screen sharing even broadcasts into the vast majority of mobile phones and tablets without requiring installs, downloads or plug-ins of any kind on virtually every browser.

Can I Initiate Screen Sharing from a Mobile Phone or Tablet?

This feature is not currently supported.

How can I Share only a Portion of my Screen while Using Screen Sharing?

If you deselect the full screen option from the Samesurf Screen Sharing modal, you'll trigger a red box that will limit the shared area that's visible to your followers. You can move the red box around by holding your cursor down and dragging it around. You can also increase or decrease its dimensions by holding your cursor down on the lower left and lower right hand portions of the box and by pulling it up, down or in a diagonal direction.

How many Followers can I Share my Screen with at the Same Time?

Maximum broadcasting levels depend on a variety of factors including the size of the screen being shared, the dynamic nature of the content and the speed of the participants' web connections. Our internal testing has shown that on high-powered machines within robust web connections, you can share your screen with up to 40 participants in a single room. To support a higher number of users, Samesurf co-browsing is recommended.

Are Data and Screen Share Transmissions Encrypted?

Yes. We utilize TLS/SSL encryption to safeguard all data transmissions and screen broadcasts.

Are Images of my Screen Passing through Samesurf's Servers during Screen Sharing and if so, does Samesurf Store or Process that Data?

The images of your screen share sessions do flow through our servers but only for the purpose of enabling your followers to view the contents of your screen. This data is never stored to disk and is cleared after your session ends.

How can I Improve the Quality of my Screen Sharing Experience?

The best way to enjoy a lag-free shared experience is to opt for Samesurf Co-Browsing. If you're sharing offline content like applications or other items that reside on your desktop, you can vastly improve the screen sharing performance by (1) sending and receiving screen broadcasts on a faster web connection (ex: cable, DSL or 4G wireless) and (2) minimizing the portion of your screen that's being shared by utilizing the red box.

Why does Samesurf Incorporate Screen Sharing in Addition to Co-Browsing?

Screen sharing is helpful when you're collaborating on apps and offline content on your desktop. If you need to use the screen sharing feature, please remember that you're sharing more than a single browser tab and that the contents of your entire screen may be visible to your followers.

Explore the Features

Will Others be able to See the Contents of my Entire Screen while I'm Co-Browsing and Screen Sharing?

Followers won't be able to see items on the leader's screen that are outside the shared browser tab during co-browsing. Our patented shared browsing technology is much different than alternatives for real-time sharing like screen sharing where the risk of exposing private content in the form of open tabs, pop-ups and passwords is significantly higher. With Samesurf co-browsing, users in a shared session follow the leader from page to page as they interact with one another and the page within a single browser tab.

During screen sharing, followers will be able to see the entire desktop or the portion that has been selected for sharing within the red box.

Will Others be able to See my Passwords as I enter this sort of Data during Co-Browsing?

On enabled sites for publishers who have opted for this option, Samesurf only passes text inputs among room participants that are typed within non-sensitive input fields. Text that is entered within properly designated password fields will be ineligible to be passed or viewed by others.

If any user types characters within a non-sensitive input box such as a search field, these input entries will be made visible to others in the room so you can all collaborate within those input boxes in real-time. Websites who integrate our technology can also independently designate other input fields as non-shareable by simply tagging these fields within their code.
